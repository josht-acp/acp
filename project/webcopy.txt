PAGE 02  ·  ABOUT
SEO Brief
Title: About — Ascension Capital Partners · Institutional Alternatives Advisory
Meta: Principal-led institutional alternatives platform. Twelve-plus years of institutional experience, USD 10bn+ cumulative transactions, global coverage.
H1
An institutional platform built for the transition economy.
Body
Ascension Capital Partners operates as a principal-led, fee-only, zero-capital institutional alternatives advisor. Our mandate is narrow and deliberate: originate, structure and syndicate institutional capital for sustainable real assets and transition infrastructure across APAC, EMEA, the Middle East and the Americas.
We operate with the discipline of an institutional investor, the documentation standards of a tier-1 investment bank, and the agility of a partnership. Every mandate is structured to LMA / LSTA credit standards, ICMA bond principles where labelled, and IFC Performance Standards where sovereign-adjacent. Every counterparty is qualified institutional. Every engagement is principal-led.
The firm was founded on the view that the binding constraint in institutional alternatives has moved from origination to execution velocity, and that a focused, principal-led platform operating at market-standard documentation grade can compete with — and often outpace — capital-first advisors operating across a broader footprint.
What We Are Not
Not a fund manager. We do not manage pooled capital.
Not a retail platform. We transact exclusively with qualified institutional counterparties.
Not a generalist. We lead with impact, transition and real assets.
Leadership
Joshua Ting, Executive Managing Partner and Global Head of Alternatives &amp; Private Markets, leads origination, structuring and distribution across the platform. Twelve-plus years of institutional experience and USD 10bn+ cumulative transactions led across UK, US, Australia, Middle East and Europe.
Entity &amp; Regulatory
Ascension Capital Partners trades as APEX Consulting Partners Pty Ltd (ACN 674 649 417). Registered in New South Wales, Australia. Governing law applied to mandate documentation is principally England &amp; Wales with arbitration under the rules of the London Court of International Arbitration.
